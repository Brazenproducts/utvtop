Overview

UTV hard tops fall into two material categories: polycarbonate and aluminum. Polycarbonate is lighter, cheaper, and translucent — it lets light into the cab and is easier to install solo. Aluminum is heavier, more expensive, more durable, and provides better heat reflection in desert conditions. Both offer meaningful protection from rain, sun, and trail debris that mesh shades and soft tops can't match. The choice between them should be based on your climate and primary use. For most riders in most conditions, polycarbonate hard tops hit the right balance of protection, weight, and cost. For desert southwest riders or those who prioritize maximum heat reflection, aluminum is worth the premium.

Buying Guide — What to Know Before You Buy

Polycarbonate Thickness Standards

3/16" (4.8mm) is the industry standard for quality UTV hard tops. 1/4" adds durability but significant weight. 1/8" is acceptable for light use but cracks more easily under debris impact. Always verify thickness in product specs.

UV Inhibitor Quality

UV coating prevents polycarbonate from yellowing and becoming brittle. Surface treatments last 2–3 years. Multi-layer UV inhibitors built into the material last 7–10+ years. Ask the manufacturer which type is used.

Heat Buildup in Closed Cabs

A solid hard top creates an enclosed cab. In summer heat, temperatures inside can reach 130–140°F when parked. Crack the doors or windows when parked in sun. Consider a ventilated roof panel if riding in extreme heat frequently.

Mounting Point Integrity

Always use the factory cage mounting points when available. Improvised mounting to cage tubing with clamps can loosen under vibration and create a dangerous projectile. Machine-specific hardware is worth the premium.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a polycarbonate UTV hard top last?

With quality UV inhibitor coating, a 3/16" polycarbonate top lasts 7–12 years under regular use. Tops stored outdoors in harsh sun without UV protectant maintenance may yellow and crack within 3–5 years.

Will a hard top void my UTV warranty?

Bolt-on accessories that don't modify the powertrain or structural components do not void UTV warranties. Always keep installation documentation showing the accessory didn't cause any claimed failure.

Do I need a windshield with a hard top?

A hard top provides overhead protection only. A windshield provides frontal protection. Running both gives complete weather protection. Running a hard top alone protects from rain but leaves you exposed to wind and rain from the front.

Can I add a light bar under a hard top?

Yes — most quality hard tops have pre-drilled mounting points or can be drilled for interior LED light bars. Rigid Industries and SuperATV both make interior light bars designed to mount to hard top inner panels.

How heavy is a polycarbonate UTV hard top?

SuperATV polycarbonate tops: 14–20 lbs depending on machine size. Aluminum tops: 25–40 lbs. This is within the structural margin of most factory UTV cage systems, but verify your machine's roof load rating.

Can a hard top be installed solo?

Yes — polycarbonate tops in the 14–20 lb range can typically be installed by one person with basic hand tools. Aluminum tops over 30 lbs benefit from a helper. Use a furniture pad on the cage to avoid scratching the top during positioning.

What maintenance does a hard top require?

Clean with mild soap and water monthly. Apply UV protectant spray (polycarbonate-specific) at the start of each riding season. Inspect mounting hardware for loosening every 5–10 hours of trail use.

Are aftermarket hard tops as good as OEM?

Premium aftermarket brands (SuperATV, Tusk) match or exceed OEM quality on most machines. OEM tops from Polaris and Can-Am are designed specifically for their machines but carry a significant price premium. For most riders, premium aftermarket is the better value.
